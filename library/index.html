Books

A short description about books I have read.


  1. Mathematics for Machine Learning — Marc Peter Deisenroth, A. Aldo Faisal, Cheng Soon Ong
    This comprehensive textbook provides an accessible introduction to the essential mathematics required for machine learning. It covers key mathematical concepts such as linear algebra, calculus, and probability, explaining their relevance and application in various machine learning algorithms. Designed for students and practitioners, this book bridges the gap between mathematical theory and its practical implementation in the field of machine learning.


  2. Introduction to Algorithms — Third Edition – Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, Clifford Stein
    This widely acclaimed and authoritative textbook, commonly known as CLRS, provides a comprehensive and rigorous introduction to algorithms. It covers fundamental design paradigms, advanced analysis techniques, and essential data structures, serving as an indispensable reference for students, researchers, and practitioners in computer science.


  3. Introduction to Algorithms: A Creative Approach — (2-Volume Set) – Udi Manber
    This comprehensive two-volume set provides a thorough exploration of fundamental algorithms and advanced design techniques, emphasizing creative problem-solving. It equips readers with a deep understanding of algorithm analysis, data structures, and computational thinking, serving as an essential resource for students and practitioners in computer science.


  4. Mathematical Problem-Solving — D. Fomin, S. Genkin, I. Itenberg
    This book introduces an acclaimed methodology for cultivating mathematical talent, rooted in the Russian educational tradition. It presents a rich collection of problems and conceptual discussions aimed at developing advanced problem-solving skills, mathematical intuition, and creative thinking, serving as an invaluable resource for aspiring Olympiad participants.


  5. Problem-Solving Strategies — Volume 1, 2 – Arthur Engel
    This book systematically introduces a wide range of powerful techniques and methodologies for advanced problem-solving strategies, particularly for mathematics competitions. It is designed to enhance analytical thinking and equip readers with approaches to tackle complex mathematical problems across various domains.



  6. Introduction to Mathematical Logic — Herbert B. Enderton
    This book offers a rigorous introduction to the foundational concepts of mathematical logic. It systematically covers propositional and first-order logic, formal systems, and key meta-mathematical theorems, providing essential insights for advanced study in mathematics, computer science, and the philosophy of logic.



  7. Number Theory for Mathematics Olympiads — Roya Beheshti Zavare and Maryam Mirzakhani
    This book provides a specialized and rigorous treatment of number theory for mathematics Olympiad preparation. It covers core concepts and advanced problem-solving techniques, essential for mastering challenging problems in this field for competitive examinations.



  8. Methods of Combinatorics — Vol. 1, 2, 3
    This comprehensive three-volume series, "Methods of Combinatorics," offers a thorough exploration of combinatorial techniques. It systematically covers foundational principles to advanced problem-solving methodologies, providing a complete resource for mastering intricate combinatorial concepts and applications across various levels of study.



  9. Calculus with Analytic Geometry — Richard A. Silverman, Vol. 1, 2, 3
    A foundational text in mathematics, this book offers a clear and rigorous introduction to calculus and analytic geometry. Silverman’s explanations build a strong conceptual understanding, essential for advanced studies in machine learning. Ideal for computer engineers.



  10. Bulletin of the Iranian Mathematical Society — Vol. 35 & 36, No. 2
    The Bulletin of the Iranian Mathematical Society is a biannual publication of the Iranian Mathematical Society in English. It publishes original research papers and invites survey articles in all area of mathematical sciences.




  11. Introduction to Graph Theory — Douglas B. West (Persian Translation)
    This book provides a structured introduction to graph theory, covering both fundamental and advanced topics such as trees, connectivity, planarity, coloring, and matching. With clear explanations and well-chosen examples, it serves as a reliable resource for students with a mathematical background.



  12. Numerical Computations for Science and Engineering Students — Dr. Asghar Karami Chegeni
    This book presents a well-organized introduction to numerical methods, designed for students in science and engineering. It covers core topics such as root-finding, numerical integration, interpolation, linear systems, and differential equations, combining theoretical insight with practical problem-solving approaches.


  13. Linear Algebra — Jin Ho Kwak, Sungpyo Hong (Persian Translation)
    This book offers a comprehensive Persian translation of a foundational text in linear algebra. It rigorously covers essential concepts such as vector spaces, linear transformations, matrices, and eigenvalues, making it an indispensable resource for advanced undergraduate and graduate students in mathematics, computer science, and engineering.


  14. Foundations of Algebra — Mohammad Mahdi Ebrahimi, Alireza Salmakar, Mozhgan Mahmoudi
    This book provides a rigorous and comprehensive introduction to fundamental algebraic concepts. It is designed for students seeking a deep understanding of abstract algebra, covering essential topics crucial for advanced studies in pure mathematics and related theoretical fields.


  15. Complex Numbers and Geometry — Liang-shin Hahn (Persian Translation by Mohammad Behrouzi)
    This book offers a rigorous exploration of complex numbers, emphasizing their intrinsic geometric properties and applications. It delves into the profound connections between complex analysis and Euclidean geometry, covering topics such as geometric transformations, inversive geometry, and conformal mappings.


  16. Selected Problems from American School Mathematics Competitions
    This book presents a curated collection and analysis of challenging problems from American school mathematics competitions. It focuses on developing advanced problem-solving strategies and critical thinking across various mathematical domains, including algebra, geometry, and number theory, serving as an invaluable resource for aspiring competitors.


  17. Methods of Algebra
    This book explores diverse methods and techniques within the field of algebra. It provides a focused approach to algebraic problem-solving, elucidating key concepts and offering methodologies essential for mastering complex algebraic structures and computations.




  18. Geometry for Mathematics Olympiads
    This book provides a focused and rigorous treatment of advanced geometric principles and problem-solving strategies. It is specifically designed to enhance the analytical skills and conceptual understanding required for success in challenging mathematics Olympiad competitions.



  19. Algebra for Mathematics Olympiads: Introductory Level
    This book provides a foundational introduction to algebraic concepts crucial for mathematics Olympiad preparation. It systematically covers essential topics such as identities, polynomials, functional equations, and inequalities, equipping students with the core principles and problem-solving strategies required for competitive mathematics at an introductory level.


  20. Combinatorial Analysis for Olympiads
    This book offers a specialized exploration of combinatorial analysis, focusing on the techniques and principles vital for mathematics Olympiad preparation. It covers advanced counting methods, permutations, combinations, and recurrence relations, developing the intricate problem-solving skills necessary for competitive mathematics.



  21. Introduction to Algebra for Mathematics Olympiads
    This book provides a foundational introduction to algebraic concepts and problem-solving strategies essential for the first stage of mathematics Olympiads. It systematically covers core topics, aiming to build a solid conceptual understanding and practical skills required for initial competitive examinations in algebra.



  22. Introduction to Algorithms for Computer Olympiads
    This book offers a foundational introduction to algorithms and computational thinking, specifically tailored for the first stage of Computer Olympiads. It covers essential algorithmic concepts and problem-solving methodologies, equipping students with the core skills required for initial competitive programming challenges.
